The Solar System

The Solar System is a gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, either directly or indirectly. Of the objects that orbit the Sun directly, the largest are the eight planets, with the remainder being smaller objects, such as dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies.

The planets in the Solar System are classified as terrestrial planets and gas giants.

Terrestrial planets include Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. Gas giants include Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.
